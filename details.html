Details

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the sun. It is terrestrial and has a thin exosphere made of particles the sun blasts off its surface. It is also the smallest planet, and the second densest, after Earth. Temperatures on its surface reach 800 degrees Fahrenheit during the day and -270 degrees Fahrenheit during the night. Its surface appears gray and has many impact craters.

Venus

Venus is terrestrial, has a very thick atmosphere mostly made of carbon-dioxide, and is the second planet from the sun. Because of its thick atmosphere, it is the hottest planet, with temperatures reaching 880 degrees Fahrenheit at its surface. From space, Venus looks white from space because its atmosphere reflects so much light, but it would look orange on the surface because of how its atmosphere filters sunlight. Venus also has impact craters, but only large ones, because small meteors burn up before reaching the surface.

Earth's Moon

Earth’s moon has a thin exosphere, a cratered, terrestrial surface, a molten interior, and a solid core. As it orbits Earth, its gravity causes the oceans of liquid water on Earth to move slightly, resulting in observable rising and falling of tides. The moon’s surface is mostly made of oxygen, silicon, magnesium, iron, calcium, and aluminum.

Mars

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere and is the fourth planet from the sun. Its crust is made of iron, magnesium, aluminum, calcium, and potassium. Mars looks red because of suspended rust particles in the atmosphere. There are dust storms occasionally.

Jupiter is a gas giant with no surface and is the fifth planet from the sun. It has rings, but they are difficult to see.
